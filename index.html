IV

Shared Backbone

Cross-utilization is the point. Five bases already feed every cuisine; a small core of staples runs through most dishes. Stock the backbone deep — then each cuisine needs only a few specialty accents, and almost nothing goes to waste.

Buy the core deep. Buy the accents as you go.

Aromatics
Garlic, onion, shallot, ginger, scallions.
Fats & acids
Olive oil, a neutral oil, butter · lemons, limes, a vinegar.
Umami
Soy, fish sauce, anchovy, canned tomato & paste, Parmesan.
Heat
Dried chilies, chili crisp, a fresh chili.
Grains & legumes
Rice, lentils, chickpeas, white & black beans — the fiber backbone.
Weekly herbs
Cilantro, parsley, mint, dill, basil — they cross every cuisine.

Make a batch, use it all week. A few make-ahead bits each cross several dishes:
· Quick-pickled red onion → carnitas, barbacoa, camarones; good on any bowl.
· Herb-yogurt / tzatziki → Greek chicken & gyro; the green goddess bowl.
· Tahini-lemon sauce → Levantine shrimp & chicken; Fresh Bowls; Greek.
· Pickled carrot & daikon → banh mi bowl, tacos, gochujang bowl.
· A pot of grains (rice, farro, lentils) → every bowl, plus Levantine, French, Spanish.

VII

The Weekly Market Run

The bases and pantries are done. Weekly, refresh produce, herbs, greens, and a tin or two of beans.

Shop brightness, not recipes.

Leafy greens
A bunch or two every trip. Asian market — gai lan, bok choy, pea shoots, yu choy, long beans. Whole Foods — kale, chard, escarole, spinach, arugula.
Fiber staples
Keep stocked: lentils, chickpeas, white & black beans, bulgur. The fiber backbone — cheap, shelf-stable, in half the dishes.
Fresh herbs
Cilantro, basil, dill, mint, parsley, scallions.
Acid & crunch
Citrus, cucumber, radish, cabbage, in-season tomato.

Asian and specialty markets handle shelf-stable pantry restocks occasionally — not weekly.
